Martin Allen

Software Engineer, The Investigative Journalism Foundation

I research civic data and build software that improves it.

I am the IJF's primary backend engineer. Today I work mostly on Open by Default, Canada's largest ever trove of government documents. I built the internal platform we use to process the messy, irregular streams of files we receive daily from more than 150 federal departments.

Earlier, while helping the IJF launch, I built the system that powers our stores of structured data. It collates disparate data sources from each province and territory into clean, useful formats. Most recently, we published a Procurement database.

I work daily with Python, PostgreSQL, Docker/Linux, and AWS. I have special experience in web scraping (e.g. this piece of work) and in data engineering.

I assist or lead the fact-finding process for all our databases. The handbook I wrote for our Procurement data describes our process and our perennial challenges.
